ABOUT

Mechanical Engineer · Space Systems · Rocket Propulsion · OpenFOAM · Additive Manufacturing · Product Design

I'm Nitesh Neupane, a mechanical engineering student at Kathmandu University who builds things — from solid rockets to prosthetic knee joints to RC planes.

My work spans aerospace and product design: I've designed and machined rocket nozzles, conducted Class-M static fire tests, and engineered pneumatic umbilical release mechanisms at Mach24 Orbitals. On the product side, I've selected suitable FDM 3D printing parameters for a polycentric knee joint and performed ISO 10328 UTM testing, reprocessed PET bottles into 3D printing filament, and led RC plane builds through the KU Robotics Club — each project driven by the same principle: thoughtful design, built to function.

I'm fluent across the full design-to-fabrication pipeline — SolidWorks, OpenFOAM, ANSYS, 3D printing, CNC machining, and composite layup. Whether it's a launch vehicle component or a human-centered assistive device, I care about getting the details right.

ISOGRID STUDIO — Parametric Rib-Grid Design Tool

2024 — Present · Open Source · Web App

Browser-based parametric isogrid/stiffener design tool for aerospace and mechanical engineering. Import STEP/STL/OBJ models, select faces, and generate isogrid, square, hex, rhombus, rectangular, or orthogrid patterns with real-time 3D preview. Includes material library, NASA presets, mass/stiffness metrics, and multi-format export (STL, STEP, OBJ). Built with Three.js.
