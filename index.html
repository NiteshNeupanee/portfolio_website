Project Highlights

Model Rocket ("Pandav")

Pandav is a mid-range rocket weighing 700g, developed to test subsystems like parachute deployment and flight datalogger. Cardboard body tube, epoxy-reinforced, powered by a G25W equivalent motor, Mach 0.32, apogee 632m.

Equatorial Mount

Mount for tracking planets and nebulae, cancelling Earth's rotation. Polar axis at 27°, concrete base for minimal vibration. Next: add motors and aluminum body.

Rocket Motor Thrust Tester V1

Device to measure thrust of solid rocket motors (up to 20kg) using a load cell and Arduino Nano. Data stored on SD card. Used for real-world vs. simulated thrust comparison.

Prototyping of SMIOS

Single Man Operated Stretch (SMIOS): a portable stretcher for emergency transport in remote areas. Designed for comfort and practicality, inspired by traditional Nepali Doko.

Project Udaya (HAB)

High altitude balloon project at Kathmandu University. Designed shock absorption structures for CanSat payloads; performed simulations for deformation and elasticity.

RC Plane Restoration

Repaired and restored a Skywalker RC plane kit. Worked on electronics, RC transmitter/receiver, flight mechanics (roll, yaw, pitch), servos, and BLDC motor.

Telescope Adapter

Custom adapter to connect telescope to EQ mount. Prototyped and 3D printed in PLA for secure, vibration-free mounting.
