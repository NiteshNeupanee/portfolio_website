About Me

Mechanical Engineer | Space Systems | Rocket Propulsion | OpenFOAM | Additive Manufacturing | Product Design

I'm Nitesh Neupane, a mechanical engineering student at Kathmandu University who builds things — from solid rockets to prosthetic knee joints to RC planes.

My work spans aerospace and product design: I've designed and machined rocket nozzles, conducted Class-M static fire tests, and engineered pneumatic umbilical release mechanisms at Mach24 Orbitals. On the product side, I've developed an additive-manufactured polycentric knee joint for children, reprocessed PET bottles into 3D printing filament, and led RC plane builds through the KU Robotics Club — each project driven by the same principle: thoughtful design, built to function.

I'm fluent across the full design-to-fabrication pipeline — SolidWorks, OpenFOAM, ANSYS, 3D printing, CNC machining, and composite layup. Whether it's a launch vehicle component or a human-centered assistive device, I care about getting the details right.
